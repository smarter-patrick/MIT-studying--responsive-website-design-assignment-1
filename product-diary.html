"Winsor & Newton Visual"

Alison Sigethy
Artist message: Living with beautiful objects that pay tribute to the natural world reminds us to slow down and helps us reconnect with nature.

"Temper Temporary"

Pete McCutchen
artist message: Looking past the obvious, close observation and engagement of the subject is my process. The challenge is to see beyond the distraction of the conspicuous to capture its unique self. Some of my subjects are quite beautiful, others less so.

"Blooming in Paris"

Dawn Benedetto
artist message: Looking past the obvious, close observation and engagement of the subject is my process. The challenge is to see beyond the distraction of the conspicuous to capture its unique self. Some of my subjects are quite beautiful, others less so.

"Circular"

Nancy McIntyre
artist message: I like it when a place has been around long enough that there is a kind of tension between the way it was originally designed to look and the way it looks now, as well as a tension between the way it looks to whoever is caring for it and the way it looks to me.

"Relaxing home"

Andy Yoder Many people take great comfort in the bathroom towels being the same color as the soap, toilet paper, and tiles. It means there is a connection between them, and an environment of order. This sense of order, acts as a shield against the unpredictability and lurking chaos of the outside world.

"Darkness in mind

Charlene Fuhrman-Schulz
artist message: Celebrate the human, the marks people make on the world. Treasure the local, the small-scale, the eccentric, the ordinary. Find the beauty in some battered old porch or cluttered, human-scale storefront, while it still stands.

"Spring time collection"

Margaret Cerutti
artist message: Capturing the light is everything! As a plein air painter, it is always the light that I remember most about any location.
